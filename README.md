# Cmos-Buffer-Amplifier-with-slew-rate-Enhancement-
This thesis presents the design, analysis, simulation, fabrication, and experimental characterization of a low-power CMOS buffer amplifier featuring slew-rate enhancement and power-saving techniques, designed for high-resolution display driver applications. Modern display systems, including organic light-emitting diode (OLED), active-matrix liquid crystal displays (AMLCDs), and micro light-emitting diode (micro-LED) panels, demand output buffer amplifiers with both high slew rates and minimal quiescent current to drive capacitive data lines accurately and efficiently.

The proposed buffer amplifier employs a wide-input-range folded cascode topology with complementary PMOS/NMOS input pairs. To dynamically enhance the driving current during transient events, cross-coupled input transistor pairs (M1--M4) implemented in low-threshold CMOS are introduced. This adaptive current-boosting scheme automatically injects extra drive current only when a large differential input voltage -- indicative of a transient -- is detected, returning to a low quiescent current state once the output settles. A positive feedback network formed by transistors M5--M8 further accelerates transient settling by augmenting the output source or sink current during rising and falling edges, respectively, while maintaining a loop gain below unity to preserve closed-loop stability. An adaptive bias-switching mechanism suppresses static power dissipation during steady-state intervals.

Simulated and measured results are in close agreement (within 5--8\%), confirming the validity of the design methodology. Process-voltage-temperature (PVT) corner simulations demonstrate that the slew rate remains above 7~V/$\mu$s under all worst-case operating conditions, validating the robustness of the design. The proposed architecture provides a practical and silicon-proven solution to the fundamental power-slew rate trade-off in display driver output buffers and establishes a methodology transferable to other high-speed, low-power analog output buffer applications.
